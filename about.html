Who We Are

CSS for All was started in the beautiful Hudson Valley in Upstate New York ten years ago - it's been a while!

Jacob Berg, Alan Blue, and Ivan Alvisovich recognized the need to bring quality, professional CSS guides to the bursting world of web design.

Meet them below!

Jacob Berg

I'm Jake!

I'm from Rhinebeck, New York and attend Binghamton University where I study math. I started programming in 2017 when I first took a C++ course at Dutchess Community College. Since then, I've known that this is my passion. I try to study a little bit of everything, but there is never enough time.

Right now, I am focusing on learning web development with Ruby on Rails as the backend.

Alan Blue

Alan Blue, here.

I've been working as a front-end developer for the last twelve years (not to date myself). When I first began, CSS was not the beautiful, established markup language that it is today - we used floats to position everything! That sucked. But now there's flexbox and grid.

Check out my tutorials on them. Using them in your webpage can save hours of time.

Ivan Alvisovich

I'm Ivan, the old one.

In my day, web development meant you hadn't cleaned your basement in a while! The first language I learned to express my thoughts in was binary - and that sucked way more than using floats to position elements on the page. Trust me.

I spend my days smoking a pipe, dressing up like I'm a pseudo-adventurer, and writing bitchin' stylesheets
